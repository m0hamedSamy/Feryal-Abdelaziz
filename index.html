Feryal Abdelaziz

Feryal Ashraf Abdelaziz is an Egyptian karateka and the first female Egyptian to have won a gold medal at the Olympic Games. She won the gold medal in the women's +61 kg event at the 2020 Summer Olympics held in Tokyo, Japan.

Feryal finished on top of Group B with five points after winning two of her matches, while losing one and drawing the other. She then had to face eventual bronze medalist Sofya Berultseva from Kazakhstan in the semi-final and came out victorious 5-4. In the final, she was up against Group A winner Irina Zaretska and managed to narrowly win 2-0 in what was a very tough encounter. Feryal’s win comes just 24 hours after Gianna Farouk’s historic achievement of winning Egypt’s first-ever medal in the karate events, with a bronze medal in the Kumite 61kg. This marks Egypt’s sixth medal at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics and the first gold medal, as the previous ones included one silver and four bronze.
